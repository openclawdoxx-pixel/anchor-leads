# Missed-Call Text-Back Pitch — "24/7 Lead Recovery Engine"

## Opener
Hey [OWNER], [YOUR NAME] — quick one. I was looking at your Google reviews and I saw a couple of people mentioned they couldn't reach you after hours. Got 30 seconds?

## Discovery
- Real talk — how many calls do you miss on a typical day when you're in the truck or on a job?
- What happens to those people? You call them back, or do they call the next plumber on Google?
- If a system texted them back in 5 seconds and booked the job for you while you were working, would that help?

## Pitch
Here's what I do. Someone calls, you don't pick up, the system instantly texts them: "Hey this is [YOUR COMPANY], saw your call — how can I help?" Then an AI handles the whole back-and-forth just like you would. Books the job, sends you the details. $0 to set up. $75 per booked job only. First 3 are on me. You literally cannot lose money on this.

## Objections
### "I already answer most calls"
Most isn't all. The ones you miss are going to your competitors — and those are usually the emergency jobs, which are the big tickets. Even 1 extra booking a week is $1,600+ a month.

### "AI can't handle plumbing questions"
It doesn't need to. It asks the same stuff you'd ask — name, address, what's broken, when they need you. Then pings you the details. You just show up and fix it.

### "What if it books something I can't do?"
You get the details first. Nothing's locked in until you confirm. And if you show up and it's a $100 tiny job, the fee vanishes — you only pay $75 on real jobs.

### "I don't trust texting customers"
Your customers are texting people all day. The ones who called you are begging to be texted back.
