# Reputation Pitch — "5-Star Reputation Shield"

## Opener
Hey [OWNER], [YOUR NAME] — quick one. I was looking you up on Google and you've got [X] reviews. Your competitors in [CITY] have 200+. That's costing you jobs every week. Got 30 seconds?

## Discovery
- Do you ever ask happy customers for reviews?
- Out of 10 satisfied customers, how many actually leave one?
- What if you had a system that texted them the review link automatically, 30 minutes after the job was done?

## Pitch
I set up automated review requests — every customer gets a text right after you leave the driveway asking for a quick Google review. Most plumbers go from 2-3 reviews a month to 15-20. More reviews means higher Google ranking, which means more calls — the whole flywheel feeds itself. Free to set up. $75 per booked job that comes in from the ranking bump. First 3 on me.

## Objections
### "I don't want to bother customers"
One text, 30 minutes after you leave. Customers who had a good experience are happy to leave a review when you ask — you just don't ask right now.

### "I already ask in person"
In-person asks get forgotten by the time they're home. A text 30 minutes later, when they're sitting down and relieved the pipe is fixed — that's when you get 5 stars.

### "What if I get a bad review?"
The system intercepts unhappy ones privately first — you get a chance to make it right before they post publicly. Only the good ones get pushed to Google.
